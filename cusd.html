Cornell Univeristy Sustainable Design

During my time at Cornell, I worked in a subdivison of an enviornmentally-focused project team called Cornell University Sustainable Design. My team, called Currents, worked toward designing and marketing a smart HVAC system that uses intelligent occupancy sensing and machine learning to reduce energy consumption. Within Cornell Engineering offices, the multi-sensor system uses a location tracking smartphone application, PIR motion sensor, and machine learning algorithm to not only determine when professors are in their offices, but when they are likely to arrive. This is key information for conserving energy because it ensures the room is heated/cooled to a comfortable temperature no sooner than when the professor actually arrives. As the lead of the thermal modeling team, I organized team meetings, conducted experiments to obtain thermal data, and used MATLAB Simulink software to determine how long it would take for model offices to heat up and cool down.
