Statistical Data Mining: Otter Tool-Use

As the culminating project for my statistical data mining course, my team and I set out to predict tool-use behavior in California sea otters. We applied supervised learning techniques such as logistic regression, k-nearest neighbors, and tree-based methods to predict when an individual otter would employ a tool to eat its prey. We also applied unsupervised learning techniques such as hierarchical clustering and k-means clustering to pick out other patterns in otter tool-use behavior.
