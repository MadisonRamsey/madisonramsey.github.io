Tremorstop Glove

In my cornerstone biomedical device design lab, I worked with three classmates to design a tremor counteracting glove for patients with Parkinsons' Disease. Our team, Seismo, used principles of torque and gyroscope physics to guide the design of our device, the Tremorstop Glove. My role consisted largely of implementing a PID feedback loop in C++ that enabled the speed of the motors within the glove to dynamically adjust, allowing the glove to dampen tremors of varying intesities in real time.
